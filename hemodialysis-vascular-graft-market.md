<p><p>Hemodialysis vascular grafts are used to create a pathway for blood flow during dialysis procedures. There are different types of vascular graft materials available, including polyester, polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), polyurethane, and biological materials. Polyester grafts are durable and provide good blood flow, while PTFE grafts are flexible and have low infection rates. Polyurethane grafts are known for their biocompatibility and strength. Biological materials, such as human umbilical vein or bovine vessels, are used for their natural compatibility with the body. Each type has its own advantages and is chosen based on individual patient needs.</p></p>
