This type is often used in power plants and industrial facilities to comply with emissions regulations. Type 2, on the other hand, may include additional technologies such as spray dry scrubbers or circulating fluidized beds to enhance sulfur dioxide removal efficiency. Type 2 systems are generally more advanced and may be preferred for larger facilities with higher emissions levels.</p></p>
