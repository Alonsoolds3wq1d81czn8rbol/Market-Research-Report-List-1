<p><p>Self-heating cans are available in various market types such as aluminum and silica, calcium oxide and water, copper sulfate and zinc, and other market types. In aluminum and silica cans, a chemical reaction between aluminum and silica generates heat. Calcium oxide and water cans use the exothermic reaction between calcium oxide and water to heat the contents. Copper sulfate and zinc cans rely on the reaction between copper sulfate and zinc to produce heat. Other market types utilize different chemical reactions to achieve self-heating capabilities in cans.</p></p>
