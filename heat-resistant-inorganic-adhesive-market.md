<p><p>Heat-resistant inorganic adhesives come in various types based on their chemical composition. Silicate-type adhesives are known for their high temperature resistance and good adhesion properties. Sulfate-type adhesives offer strong bonding capabilities and resistance to heat. Phosphate-type adhesives are commonly used for high-temperature applications due to their excellent thermal stability. Borate-type adhesives are preferred for their high strength and resistance to chemicals. Other types of heat-resistant inorganic adhesives include aluminate and zirconate adhesives, each offering unique properties for specific applications.</p></p>
