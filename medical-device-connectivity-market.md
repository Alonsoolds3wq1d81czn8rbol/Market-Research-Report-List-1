<p><p>The medical device connectivity market can be classified into two main types: wireless and wired connectivity. Wireless connectivity involves the use of wireless networks to seamlessly transmit data from medical devices to electronic medical records (EMRs) or other healthcare systems, allowing for real-time monitoring and analysis. On the other hand, wired connectivity involves the physical connection of medical devices to EMRs or healthcare systems through wired interfaces such as Ethernet or USB cables, enabling the transfer of data. Both wireless and wired connectivity options play a crucial role in improving healthcare efficiency and patient outcomes.</p></p>
