<p><p>Alfalfa forage has various market types, including dried pulp and wet pulp. Dried pulp is made by drying alfalfa to reduce moisture content, making it easier to store and transport. It is commonly used as animal feed. Wet pulp, on the other hand, is alfalfa that has been ensiled with a higher moisture content. This type of forage is typically used as a feed ingredient for livestock. Both dried and wet pulp markets play a crucial role in supplying high-quality forage for animals.</p></p>
